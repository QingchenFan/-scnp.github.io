ABOUT LAB

The Laboratory of Social Cognitive Neuroscience in Psychiatry (SCNP), also known as the Brain-Mind-Society SCNP, was established in March 2022. It is the first research group with a joint principal investigator (PI) organizational structure in the Institute of Psychology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. The group is co-led by two researchers, Professor Gu Ruolei and Professor Zhou Yuan, who share similar research interests and complementary expertise. The SCNP focuses on the study of social cognitive deficits in individuals with psychological disorders, such as depression, and aims to investigate the underlying psychological mechanisms and biological basis of these deficits. The ultimate goal is to establish assessment criteria for social cognitive symptoms and develop intervention methods with practical applications.

Research Interests

Gu Ruolei

The impact of trait emotions and emotional disorders on interpersonal interaction from a temporal perspective and their neural mechanisms.

Currently, I am actively expanding my research focus to the domain of human-computer interaction, specifically in the areas of social information processing, moral judgment, and emotional experiences.

Zhou Yuan

The neurobiological basis of social cognitive impairments in mental disorders such as depression and schizophrenia is being investigated using functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) techniques.

The aim is to understand the neural mechanisms underlying aberrant behaviors in individuals with mental disorders and to identify biomarkers for early diagnosis and prognosis assessment of these conditions.

Research Interests: Integrating the use of behavioral and neuroimaging experiments, combined with machine learning and other methods to explore the association between abnormal changes in brain networks and their aberrant behaviors in patients with psychiatric disorders
